Database Administrators (DBA's)


Description...

Database administrators use specialized software to store and organize data, such as financial information and customer shipping records. They make sure that data are available to users and are secure from unauthorized access, capacity planning, installation, configuration, database design, migration, performance monitoring, security, troubleshooting, and backup/data recovery.

Salary...

In 2015, the median pay for database administrators was $39.29 per hour, and $81,710 per year. The typical entry-level education, is a bachelor's degree in an information or computer related subject such as computer science. The work experience required in a related occupation, is less than 5 years. Many DBAs have had experience in programming. It is also a good idea to familiarise yourself with as many database technologies and operating systems as possible. Most DBA positions require knowledge of structured query language (SQL), Unix, and database management systems (DBMS).

Hours...

Working hours are usually 37 to 40 hours a week, Monday to Friday and typically include regular unsocial hours. Overnight and weekend work is often necessary as maintenance and development work needs to be undertaken during periods of low usage. They are often on call if a critical problem occurs.

Skills...

You will need to show:

  1. Communication, teamwork, and negotiation skills
  2. Problem-solving and good analytical skills
  3. Familiarity with the main data manipulation languages and the principles of database design
  4. Flexibility and adaptability
  5. The skill to work well under pressure and timed instances
  6. A commitment to continuing professional development (CPD)
