Walter Disney

Walter Elias Disney was born on December 5, 1901 in Illinois. His father and mother were Elias and Flora Disney and he had 3 brothers and a sister. After he was born, his family moved to Missouri where he grew up with an interest in art, nature, and a love for family. In 1918 he enlisted for military service, only to be turned down as he was underage. He then joined Red Cross and was sent to France where he spent a year driving an ambulance covered with Disney cartoons. Returning from France, he took up an interest in commercial art and started a small company which went bankrupt. He headed to Hollywood and was recognized for his cartoons. In 1925 he married Lillian Bounds and would go on to have two daughters. He then went on to win Academy Awards, pioneer animated cartoons, oversee the prodiction of Disneyland, and plan out Disneyworld.
